# STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION CHECKLIST

## GENERAL SAFETY TIPS
1. Use a strong, unique password for each account. Use a password manager if needed.
2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for email, banking and social media.
3. Update software regularly to fix security vulnerabilities.
4. Be cautious of links and attachments in emails or messages, even if they seem familiar.
5. Backup your data on external drives or cloud platforms.
6. Use antivirus and firewall software and keep them updated

## Prevention in Financial Transaction
7. Don't share OTP or PINs even with people claiming to be bank officials.
8. Verify the source of messages asking for urgent fund transfers.
9. Enable transaction alerts for every debit or credit made from your account.
10. Use official apps and websites; avoid clicking on unsolicited payment links.
11. Report fraud immediately to your bank and to the National Cyber Crime Portal.

## Prevention on the Personal Level
12. Don't overshare on social media, especially location, financial status, or personal identifiers. 
13. Use privacy settings to control who sees your content online.
14. Block and report cyber stalkers or online bullies.
15. Be cautious with dating apps and unknown online contacts
16. Regularly Google your name to monitor your digital footprint.
17. Talk to trusted people if you experience online harassment. It's not something you need to face alone.

## Government and legal support 
1. https://cybercrime.gov.im - for reporting incidents
2. CERT-In(Indian Computer Emergency Response Team) - for technical alerts and advisories
